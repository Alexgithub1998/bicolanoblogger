Bicolano Cuisine Gallery

Explore the rich and spicy flavors of traditional Bicol dishes

Bicol Express

Spicy pork stew in coconut milk with chili peppers

Bicol Express is an iconic Filipino dish that originated in the Bicol region. This creamy, spicy stew combines tender pork, coconut milk, shrimp paste, and an abundance of chili peppers. The dish perfectly represents Bicolano cuisine's love for spicy food and coconut milk.

Pinangat

Taro leaves wrapped with fish/pork in spicy coconut milk

Pinangat is a traditional Bicolano delicacy made by wrapping shrimp or fish in taro leaves, then cooking them in coconut milk with chilies. The leaves impart a unique flavor while the coconut milk creates a rich, creamy sauce. This dish is often served during special occasions.

Kinunot

Shredded shark meat or stingray in spicy coconut milk

Kinunot is a unique Bicolano dish that uses shark meat or stingray flakes cooked in coconut milk with malunggay leaves. The meat is carefully prepared to remove any strong flavors, resulting in a delicate yet spicy dish that showcases the region's coastal cuisine.

Laing

Dried taro leaves cooked in coconut milk and chili

Laing is a beloved Bicolano dish made from dried taro leaves simmered in coconut milk with chili peppers. The slow cooking process allows the leaves to absorb the flavors, resulting in a creamy, spicy, and aromatic dish that pairs perfectly with rice.
