"Island-Hopping and Fresh Feasts: A Memorable Adventure in Caramoan, Camarines Sur"

June 15, 2024 Alex Balaoro
Nestled in the heart of Camarines Sur, Caramoan is a hidden paradise famous for its breathtaking landscapes, white-sand beaches, and stunning marine life. My journey to Caramoan was nothing short of magical, marked by a series of island-hopping adventures and the freshest seafood I've ever tasted. We started our island-hopping escapade early in the morning, setting sail from Paniman Beach, where our bangka (small boat) waited. The moment we reached the first island, Matukad, I was captivated by its crystal-clear waters and powdery white sand. Climbing up the limestone cliffs to get a panoramic view of the secluded lagoon was exhilarating. We also visited Lahos Island, known for its sandbar and rock formations. But the highlight was Hunongan Cove, where I felt like I was transported to a different world—calm turquoise waters surrounded by lush greenery.
